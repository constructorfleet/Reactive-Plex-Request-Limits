Request Limit Update

We are adding a new request-limit system for Plex requests. If you request movies or shows and then do not watch a meaningful amount of them, your request limits may temporarily go down. If you watch what you requested, your limits recover automatically.

This is not meant to punish normal use. It is meant to stop cases where someone requests a lot of content, barely watches any of it, and fills up storage or server time that could be used for everyone else.

Did you know...

We have a lot of content available, but storage and server time are not cheap.
  • It costs us close to $500 a month to operate our media server.
  • When we run low on storage space, it can affect the performance and availability of content.
  • To upgrade our server's storage capacity, it requires significant investment - close to a hundred hours of our time and up to $18,000 of our own money.
  • We greatly appreciate the donations from our community that help us keep the server running but they don't even cover half a month's electric bill.
  • We want to make sure everyone has a good experience and can request what they actually want to watch while respecting our time, energy and finances.

How it works

When you request something, you get time to start watching it before it affects your limits.

  • For movies, you generally need to watch most (around 70%) of the movie.
  • For TV seasons, you need to watch a reasonable portion of the season.
  • The more episodes in a season, the more watched episodes are required.

If the system sees that you requested something and did not watch enough of it after that grace period, your request limits may be reduced for a while.

Examples

  • If you request The Dark Knight and do not watch it after a couple of weeks, your movie request limit may go down.
  • If you request Black Bird - Season 1, which has 10 episodes, you may need to watch around 3 episodes for it to count.
  • If you request Pokemon - Season 1, which has 70 episodes, you may need to watch around 14 episodes for it to count.

How to get your limits back

Your limits come back automatically when either:

  1. You watch enough of the movies or shows you requested.
  2. You stop making unused requests and wait long enough for the old unused requests to stop counting.

Watching what you requested is the fastest way to restore your limits.

The goal is simple: request what you actually plan to watch, and everyone keeps reasonable request access.
